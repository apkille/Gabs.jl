Suggested Readings and References

Tutorials on Gaussian Quantum Information

  • Adesso, G.; Ragy, S. and Lee, A. R. (2014). Continuous Variable Quantum Information: Gaussian States and Beyond. Open Systems & Information Dynamics 21, 1440001.
  • Olivares, S. (2011). Quantum optics in the phase space. The European Physical Journal Special Topics 203, 3–24.
  • Wang, X.-B.; Hiroshima, T.; Tomita, A. and Hayashi, M. (2007). Quantum information with Gaussian states. Physics Reports 448, 1–111.
  • Weedbrook, C.; Pirandola, S.; Garcı́a-Patrón, R.; Cerf, N. J.; Ralph, T. C.; Shapiro, J. H. and Lloyd, S. (2012). Gaussian quantum information. Rev. Mod. Phys. 84, 621–669.

Tutorials and Books on Quantum Continuous Variables

  • Braunstein, S. L. and van Loock, P. (2005). Quantum information with continuous variables. Rev. Mod. Phys. 77, 513–577.
  • Cerf, N. J.; Leuchs, G. and Polzik, E. S. (2007). Quantum Information with Continuous Variables of Atoms and Light (Imperial College Press).
  • Serafini, A. (2023). Quantum Continuous Variables: A Primer of Theoretical Methods (CRC Press).
  • Walschaers, M. (2021). Non-Gaussian Quantum States and Where to Find Them. PRX Quantum 2, 030204.

Technical Reviews on Quantum Continuous Variables

  • Andersen, U.; Leuchs, G. and Silberhorn, C. (2010). Continuous-variable quantum information processing. Laser & Photonics Reviews 4, 337–354.
  • Cerf, N. J. and Grangier, P. (2007). From quantum cloning to quantum key distribution with continuous variables: a review (Invited). J. Opt. Soc. Am. B 24, 324–334.
  • Fukui, K. and Takeda, S. (2022). Building a large-scale quantum computer with continuous-variable optical technologies. Journal of Physics B: Atomic, Molecular and Optical Physics 55, 012001.
  • Goldberg, A. Z.; Klimov, A. B.; Grassl, M.; Leuchs, G. and Sánchez-Soto, L. L. (2020). Extremal quantum states. AVS Quantum Science 2, 044701.
  • Jankowski, M.; Yanagimoto, R.; Ng, E.; Hamerly, R.; McKenna, T. P.; Mabuchi, H. and Fejer, M. M. (2024). Ultrafast second-order nonlinear photonics—from classical physics to non-Gaussian quantum dynamics: a tutorial. Adv. Opt. Photon. 16, 347–538.
  • van Loock, P. (2002). Quantum Communication with Continuous Variables. Fortschritte der Physik 50, 1177–1372.
  • Lvovsky, A. I. and Raymer, M. G. (2009). Continuous-variable optical quantum-state tomography. Rev. Mod. Phys. 81, 299–332.
  • Pirandola, S. and Mancini, S. (2006). Quantum teleportation with continuous variables: A survey. Laser Physics 16, 1418–1438.

Publications on Gaussian Quantum Measurements

  • Cenni, M. F.; Lami, L.; Acín, A. and Mehboudi, M. (2022). Thermometry of Gaussian quantum systems using Gaussian measurements. Quantum 6, 743.
  • Kiukas, J. and Schultz, J. (2013). Informationally complete sets of Gaussian measurements. Journal of Physics A: Mathematical and Theoretical 46, 485303.
  • Marco G. Genoni, L. L. and Serafini, A. (2016). Conditional and unconditional Gaussian quantum dynamics. Contemporary Physics 57, 331–349.
  • Zhang, J. (2018). Quantum measurement and preparation of Gaussian states. Ph.D. Thesis, Aarhus University.
